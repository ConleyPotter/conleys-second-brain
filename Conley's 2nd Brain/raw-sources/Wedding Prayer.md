I've been working on the wedding prayer that Olivia, our wedding officiant, will read at the ceremony. Here's a working draft below. I think it's beautiful so far.

We gather here today not just to witness a marriage,  
but to honor the story that has brought Conley and Sam to this moment.

A story shaped by chance and choice,  
by laughter, growth, and the quiet, steady decision  
to keep choosing one another.

May the love that found you continue to guide you.  
Not just in the easy moments,  
but in the ones that ask more of you:  
more patience, more honesty, more grace.

May you never lose sight of what first drew you together,  
but also never stop discovering who each other is becoming.

Let your partnership be a place of both shelter and expansion:  
a home you return to,  
and a life you build outward, side by side.

May you carry each other through seasons of uncertainty,  
celebrate one another in seasons of joy,  
and remain curious, even years from now,  
about the person standing beside you.

And may the love you share ripple outward,  
into your family, your friendships, your community,  
becoming something larger than yourselves,  
something that quietly shapes the world around you.

What you begin here today is not just a promise,  
but a living story.

May you write it well,  
together.

# Dinner Speech & Prayer 
## *this one would be read by me*

Before we begin, I just want to take a moment and look around this room.

Today has been a lot—in the best way.  
A lot of movement, a lot of emotion, a lot of meaning.  
And now we finally get to pause. To be here together.

With people we love,  
with food in front of us,  
and with this new chapter already beginning to take shape around us.

Earlier today, Sam and I made our vows.  
But a marriage isn’t built in a single moment like that.

It’s built in moments like this—  
around tables, in conversation, in laughter,  
in the simple act of showing up for one another over and over again.

And looking around this room…  
we’re really aware that none of this exists in isolation.

Every person here has played some role  
in shaping who we are, and what we’re building together.

So before we eat, I want to take a moment to acknowledge that—and to give thanks.

Let’s pray.

God, we’re grateful for this day.  
For everything that brought us here,  
and for the love that we get to step into more fully now.

We’re grateful for the people in this room—  
for family, for friendships, for the support and care  
that have carried us to this moment.

We ask that You bless this time together.  
Let it be easy, joyful, and full of connection.

And as Sam and I move forward from here,  
we ask for guidance—  
to build a life that reflects something good, something steady, something real.

A life that’s not just meaningful to us,  
but that has a positive impact on the people around us.

And we ask that moments like this—  
simple, shared, and full—  
would be something we return to again and again in the years ahead.

Amen.
