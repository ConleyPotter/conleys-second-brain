## What This Reveals

**Tilson's dropping is a signal about deal hygiene.** The "Decision Maker Bought-In" stage in HubSpot is not a guarantee — it just means the internal champion is aligned. The actual close requires the shop owner to stay engaged, and when that engagement cools (as it did with Tilson's), the deal is at risk. The mutual decision to walk away — rather than continue chasing — reflects operational maturity. This is how you protect close rate and avoid the pipeline becoming a graveyard of zombie deals.

**Mechanics of West Allis booking + Jon saying the schedule is "slammed" is a capacity signal.** Combined with the Metric Motors sales deck in progress and a new Kidders Repair prospect, BA appears to be in a high-volume growth phase in April. The question worth tracking: does the constraint become onboarding bandwidth or ongoing service delivery bandwidth? The FLACA conference, with Conley out Apr 16–20, adds to the timing pressure.

**The Brenner Newman promotion request hints at broader market seasonality.** An established client suddenly pushing promotional pricing suggests softness at the shop level — whether from weather, economic pressure, or seasonal lull. Conley's "even he's feeling it now" is worth watching. If this pattern appears across multiple clients in April/May recaps, it would have implications for BA's renewal and upsell motion.

**The conference cadence (TEKTONIC → FLACA within two weeks) reveals that BA has an active field sales and industry-presence strategy.** Adam and Jon at TEKTONIC, Conley at FLACA — these are two different events in different markets in the same stretch. BA is not just inbound; they show up. This is worth cross-referencing with [[ba-partners]] and [[ba-clients-pipeline]] to see if conference contacts appear later as referrals or new clients.

**HubSpot AI adoption is moving from individual to systemic.** Conley has been using it for post-call summaries; now Micah is confirming it can replace a workflow step (assign to client success). This is a compounding automation story inside BA's ops — worth tracking whether it reduces Conley's administrative burden or creates new leverage points for his ACE automation interests.
