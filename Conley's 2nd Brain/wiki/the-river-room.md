# The River Room

**Related:** [[conley-potter]], [[domain-personal]], [[operating-doctrine-2026]], [[financial-projections]], [[brand-voice]], [[career-history]], [[the-sentinel]]

---

## What It Is

The River Room is a planned creative commons and digital workshop to be established in a restored historic building in Marietta, Pennsylvania. It is Conley's long-range vision for a physical community institution — not an ACE side project, but the thing ACE is building toward making possible.

**Status:** Draft business plan (V2, 2026). Not active. Pre-Phase 0. No building acquired, no capital committed. The plan represents a serious, fundable concept, not a daydream.

---

## The Three-Layer Model

The River Room operates on three interlocking layers that create a self-sustaining ecosystem:

| Layer | Name | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Front of house** | The Commons | Café and curated bookshop. Open to all. Drives daily foot traffic and community belonging. |
| **Middle layer** | The Studio | Membership-based creative co-working space for writers, artists, and makers. Quiet zones, art tables, natural light. |
| **Revenue engine** | The Workshop | Digital and AI skills co-op. 6–10 workstations. Recurring workshop series + corporate training. Highest-margin stream. |

The Commons draws people in. The Studio builds community. The Workshop generates the recurring revenue that makes the mission sustainable.

---

## Location and Space

**Target:** Downtown Marietta, PA — a historic commercial structure (brick, original windows, high ceilings). 3,000–5,000 sq ft across two floors. The act of restoring a historic building is itself a statement of values: the bones of 19th-century commerce become the container for 21st-century community.

**Why Marietta:** Small river town (pop. ~2,600) at the intersection of several dynamics — remote workers and artists arriving from metros, AT corridor trail tourism, no dedicated creative/co-working space in the area, underserved local tradespeople, and strong PA arts grant infrastructure. There is no direct competitor within 15–25 miles.

## Programming

### Recurring Workshop Series (The Workshop)

- **AI for Humans** (monthly, 2-hr): Prompt craft, creative AI tools, building without coding. For writers, artists, small business owners.
- **Digital Clinic** (bi-weekly, drop-in): One-on-one office hours on websites, social media, automation basics. Co-branded with AutoBoost.
- **Tools Not Toys** (quarterly intensive): Half-day on integrating AI into creative practice without losing voice or identity.
- **River Writers Workshop** (monthly): Craft-focused writing group with optional digital publishing component.
- **Nature Journal & Field Sketching** (seasonal): Outdoors on the Susquehanna. Connects to conservation partnerships.
- **Zine Lab** (monthly): Design and print a small publication. Community storytelling via risograph or laser print.

### Corporate & Organizational Training

Packaged workshops for local employers, nonprofits, regional orgs. 3-hour to full-day formats. AI literacy, digital marketing fundamentals, creative thinking for teams. **Highest-margin revenue stream.**

### Community Programming

Monthly community conversations (climate, conservation, recovery, creativity). Quarterly author readings. Annual **River Room Creative Summit** — one-day gathering of makers, writers, and digital thinkers from across the Susquehanna Valley.

## Financial Model — The Dual-Operation Design

V2 of the business plan introduces a critical structural shift: **The River Room now runs two parallel operations** — a physical business and a digital revenue operation — as distinct legal entities sharing a unified mission.

> The digital business funds and de-risks the physical one. The physical space no longer needs to be profitable from day one. It needs to be *viable* — covering its own operating costs — while the digital operation generates the surplus capital that funds growth.

**Why this matters:** This is the same model Conley uses with ACE. The plan explicitly names "digital revenue pre-accumulation (Years 1–2 before physical opening)" as a funding source of $40K–$80K directed to the building fund. ACE is the mechanism that makes this real.

### Funding Strategy

- Personal investment / founder equity: $30K–$50K
- SBA 7(a) small business loan: $80K–$150K
- Digital revenue pre-accumulation: $40K–$80K (directed to building fund)
- Pennsylvania Council on the Arts — Cultural Facility Grant: $15K–$40K
- Lancaster County Community Foundation: $5K–$20K
- Community crowdfunding: $10K–$25K
- Historic preservation tax credits (if applicable): up to 20% reduction in restoration cost

## Phased Launch Strategy

### Phase 0 — Proof of Concept (Months 1–6)
Building-independent. Costs almost nothing. Proves demand before capital commitment.
- Host River Room pop-up workshops in borrowed spaces (libraries, churches, partner venues)
- Launch newsletter and minimal social presence
- Run 2–3 AI for Humans workshops and a River Writers session
- Begin building search in parallel
- Apply for initial PA arts grants

### Phase 1 — Open the Commons (Months 7–18)
Lease or acquire the building. Open café and bookshop. Begin soft programming. Launch studio memberships at founding member rate.

### Phase 2 — Activate the Workshop (Months 12–24)
Open the AI/digital workshop space with a launch event. Launch corporate training. Hire part-time Studio Coordinator. Establish the first annual Creative Summit.

### Phase 3 — Deepen and Sustain (Year 3+)
Evaluate transition to worker cooperative or nonprofit-hybrid model. Expand residency programming with named fellowships. Consider satellite model in Columbia, Elizabethtown, or another river town. Produce a River Room zine/chapbook series.

## Connection to ACE and The Sentinel

The River Room occupies the same structural position in Conley's thinking as [[the-sentinel]]: a meaningful long-range project that ACE revenue is intended to seed. Where The Sentinel is individual and technological, The River Room is communal and physical. Together they represent the two poles of the longer vision: the tool you build for yourself, and the place you build for everyone.

The V2 financial model is explicit: ACE-scale digital revenue (the Workshop's online equivalent) is what makes the physical space viable without grant dependency.
