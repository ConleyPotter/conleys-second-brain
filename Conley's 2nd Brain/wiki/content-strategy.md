# Content Strategy

**Related:** [[personal-brand-engine]], [[brand-voice]], [[thought-leader-engine]], [[building-out-loud]], [[portfolio-projects]], [[career-transition-strategy]]

---

## Purpose

This page defines the channel-by-channel content strategy for the Personal Brand Engine. It replaces [[campaign-plan]] (archived May 2026), which was focused on Upwork contract acquisition.

The goal is not reach for its own sake — it's building a specific reputation: *marketing technology and AI implementation leader who actually builds systems.* Every piece of content is evaluable against that lens.

## Channel Overview

| Channel | Frequency | Primary Goal | Voice energy |
|---|---|---|---|

## LinkedIn

### What to post

**Build stories (40%):** Real implementation stories from BA work (sanitized — no client names or identifying details), PBE/ACE experiments, and open source projects. Not theory — specific problems, specific solutions, specific results. "I built X because Y was painful. Here's what it actually looks like."

**MarTech / AI takes (35%):** Reactions to industry news, product releases, and trends in AI, marketing technology, and RevOps. Opinionated but grounded. Not summaries of press releases — actual takes after using the thing or watching the market move.

**Career / building in public (25%):** The transition story, real tensions (limited time, wedding year, BA workload), what it looks like to claw toward a better career position. This is differentiating content — most professionals don't narrate this in real time.

### Format
- Lead with a specific, concrete first line — not a question, not starting with "I"
- 3–7 short paragraphs
- No hashtags in body text; 2–3 in first comment if at all
- No carousels until there's a specific reason for one
- Aim for posts that a hiring manager would read fully, not just skim

### What NOT to post
- "5 things I learned about X" listicles
- Engagement bait questions
- Productivity advice or self-help content
- Anything that sounds like a newsletter pitch
- AutoBoost or BA client-identifying details

### The audience separation update
The prior strict LinkedIn/ACE separation (LinkedIn = AutoBoost only, ACE work never surfaces) has been retired. LinkedIn is now the primary career transition channel. MarTech and AI content — including references to building AI systems independently — belongs on LinkedIn. The only remaining separation: never name BA clients, never post content that would embarrass BA or conflict with the employment relationship.

## X (Twitter)

**What to post:**
- Short takes on AI tools and releases as they happen
- Building-in-public threads: "I just shipped X — here's the architecture in 6 tweets"
- Reactions to things being read
- Honest takes when something didn't work
- Amplifications of LinkedIn content (repurpose, don't just cross-post)

**Format:**
- Threads over single long tweets when there's real depth
- Real-time reactions are fine and valuable
- Less polished than LinkedIn — faster, more direct
- Quote-tweet with commentary rather than just retweeting

## Substack

Substack is the credibility backbone — longer, more technical, more permanent. Each piece should be good enough that a hiring manager would feel differently about a candidate after reading it.

### Article types

**"Here's what I actually built":** Deep-dives on a project — the problem, the architecture, what broke, what worked. The Upwork pipeline open source project (see [[portfolio-projects]]) is Article #1.

**"Here's what this technology actually does":** Clear-eyed analysis of an AI tool or platform change. Not a summary of the announcement — an actual take after using it or studying it seriously.

**"Here's what this pattern is":** Something noticed across multiple MarTech or RevOps implementations that hasn't been written about well elsewhere.

### Format
- 800–2,000 words; longer if the technical depth warrants it
- Code snippets, architecture diagrams, and workflow screenshots welcome
- End with "what I still don't know" rather than a tidy conclusion — consistent with [[brand-voice]]

### First article candidates
1. "I built an AI system that finds jobs, writes proposals, and designs its own fulfillment plans" (the Upwork pipeline story)
2. "What 8 years of building MarTech stacks taught me about AI integration"
3. "Why RevOps is the most AI-resilient role in B2B marketing right now"

## Portfolio Blog

One polished, evergreen piece per month on the personal portfolio website. These are the articles sent to a hiring manager. More craft, more editing, more permanent than Substack.

Repurpose and elevate the best Substack pieces here. The blog is the curated archive; Substack is the publishing cadence.

## Audio / Speaking

Format is TBD. See [[building-out-loud]] for full format evaluation. Key decisions pending:
- Solo vs. interview format
- Show name (Building Out Loud is a working title)
- Production overhead acceptable given time constraints

**Recommended starting approach:** Guest appearances on existing podcasts first (zero overhead, immediate audience access). LinkedIn Audio events to test the solo format. Decide on a show format once there's a feel for what's sustainable.

From a content strategy perspective, audio output feeds the same processing pipeline as written captures — podcast transcripts seed LinkedIn posts, X threads, and Substack drafts. The relationship is bidirectional.

## GitHub

See [[github-strategy]] for the full open source and contribution plan.

From a content strategy perspective: every shipped project generates at minimum one Substack article, a LinkedIn series (3–4 posts), and several X threads. GitHub activity is a content trigger, not just a coding activity. Set the rule: any commit that ships a meaningful feature gets an X thread the same day.

## The TLE as Production Layer

The [[thought-leader-engine]] processes raw captures into platform-adapted drafts. This strategy defines what to publish; the TLE handles drafting overhead so the bottleneck is a 60-second voice note, not two hours of writing.

### Weekly rhythm target (~5–7 hrs/week total)

| Day | Activity | Time |
|---|---|---|
| Monday | 3–5 captures or voice notes → TLE processes into LinkedIn/X drafts | 30 min |
| Wednesday | Review, edit, approve LinkedIn posts for the week | 30–45 min |
| Friday | Writing session: Substack draft, blog post, or project work | 60–90 min |
| Weekend | Project build time (optional but high-value) | Variable |

## Voice Consistency

All content evaluable against [[brand-voice]]:
- Does it sound like a real person talking?
- Is the intensity present but not loud?
- Am I showing the actual work, not packaging it as lessons?
- Is it technical but accessible?

The TLE SOUL.md is the machine-readable version of this guide. They must stay in sync — if brand-voice.md is updated, SOUL.md gets updated too.
