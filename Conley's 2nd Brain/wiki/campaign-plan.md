# Phase I Campaign Plan — "First Contract"

**Related:** [[ace-overview]], [[client-acquisition]], [[brand-voice]], [[upwork-portfolio]], [[platform-comparison]], [[conley-potter]]

---

## Campaign Summary

**Name:** First Contract  
**Window:** April 14 – July 13, 2026 (90 days)  
**Operator:** Conley Potter  
**Bandwidth:** 15 hrs/week (all-in: fulfillment + marketing + operations)  
**Status:** Pre-launch as of April 14, 2026

**Primary objective:** 5 completed Upwork contracts with 5-star reviews within 90 days.

**Secondary objectives:**
- Portfolio website live before first proposal batch
- 30-post Threads archive documenting the build-in-public journey
- 3-email post-delivery retention sequence in place before first contract closes
- Fiverr gig live as a passive secondary acquisition channel

---

## Target Audiences

### Primary — Upwork B2B Buyers
Marketing agencies, fractional CMOs, RevOps consultants, and SDR teams who need verified contacts fast and can't justify a full-time data analyst. They're on Upwork because they've already decided to outsource. The proposal is the conversion event.

**Pain points:** ZoomInfo/Apollo lists are expensive and stale; in-house research steals SDR selling time; they've been burned by garbage CSVs from overseas contractors.

### Secondary — Threads (Builders / Freelancers)
People building something on the side. They don't buy from ACE. They amplify content and become the referral network for Phase II/III. Phase I Threads strategy: plant seeds, not harvest.

### Audience Separation Protocol

| Channel | Audience | What They Know |
|---|---|---|
| Upwork / Fiverr / Portfolio | B2B buyers | A skilled lead research specialist who delivers verified CSVs |
| Threads | Builders / Freelancers | Someone building systems-driven freelance work in the open |
| LinkedIn | AutoBoost ecosystem | A digital marketing executive building a B2B marketing automation product |
| Email | Past / active clients | A reliable service provider who checks in |

The automation stack is **never** disclosed to clients in any channel. LinkedIn is **entirely separate** — ACE work does not surface there.

## Core Messaging

**Client-facing:** "Verified B2B contact data, structured for your CRM, built around the exact titles and geographies you actually need."

**Supporting messages:**
- Quality signal: "Every contact is verified against live data before delivery."
- Speed signal: "Most 500-contact lists delivered within 48–72 hours of order confirmation."
- Specificity signal: "I build lists around the titles you actually target — not generic job categories."
- Risk reduction: "Fixed price. No scope creep. You know what you're getting before we start."

## Channel Strategy

### Upwork (Primary — 3–4 hrs/week)
- Profile rewrite before any proposals go out (profile is the landing page)
- Initial rate: $350–450 for 500-contact list (below market to win first 2–3 reviews)
- Rate increase after 5 reviews
- Target job posts: budgets $300–700 with clear deliverable scope
- Do not apply to vague "lead generation" posts that imply ongoing outreach

### Portfolio Website (1 hr/month after setup)
- Single page: service, process, sample output (anonymized CSV), CTA to Upwork
- Focus: B2B lead enrichment, mid-Atlantic industrial/manufacturing niche
- Not a primary conversion path in Phase I — a credibility anchor

### Threads (2–3 hrs/week)
- 3 posts/week: Build post + Economics/Stakes post + Personal/Reflective post
- Write in Conley's brand voice: grounded, specific, no hustle culture
- Never pitch. Never reveal the automation stack. Document.
- See [[brand-voice]] for voice guidelines

### LinkedIn (0 incremental effort)
- Remains AutoBoost-only. ACE Phase I work does not surface here. Hard separation.
- Context: Conley appears heavily in AutoBoost's video content (in-house studio). His LinkedIn is a public-facing professional identity tied to BA's ~15-person agency and its automotive repair shop client base. Any ACE content on LinkedIn risks conflating identities in front of clients and colleagues.
- See [[conley-potter]] — Day Job section.

### Fiverr (30 min/week after setup)
- One gig: "Verified B2B Lead List — 500 Contacts, Custom Targeting"
- Passive inbound from launch day forward
- Entry pricing: $150–$250 for initial reviews

### Email (30 min/week after setup)
- Collect email from every client at intake
- 3-email post-delivery sequence: Day 0 (delivery + check-in), Day 7 (did it work?), Day 30 (capacity follow-up)
- Plain text, no HTML template — sounds like a person, not a newsletter

## Risks and Mitigations

**Risk 1: New profile has low Upwork search visibility**  
→ Front-load proposal volume in Weeks 2–4. Accept first 2–3 contracts below target rate to start the review flywheel.

**Risk 2: Bandwidth compression — fulfillment and marketing compete for the same 15 hrs**  
→ Batch Threads content weekly (one writing session = 3 posts). Email sequence built once, runs automatically. Portfolio is a one-time build.

**Risk 3: Early review quality risk — one 3-star review damages profile at a critical moment**  
→ Apply Spartan Tone Protocol strictly. Under-promise, over-deliver on first 3 contracts. Clarify scope in writing before accepting. Proactively offer revision or refund if a contract is heading toward negative outcome — protecting the profile is worth more than the contract value in Phase I.
