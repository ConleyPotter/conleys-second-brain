## The Goal

Land a high-compensation corporate or enterprise role — hybrid in Philadelphia or fully remote — within 6–12 months. Rise once or twice (Director → VP, or equivalent). Exit in 2–4 years to start a venture full-time. The job should be chosen for what it *builds* — credentials, network, equity, and domain expertise that compounds toward the eventual exit.

**Compensation target:** $130–175K base at entry, path to $150–200K+ after first promotion.

**Key personal constraints:**
- Adam and Micah have confirmed Conley can use whatever title he wants for external purposes. "Director of Client Success & Strategy" at Business Actualization is fair game.
- Security clearance eligibility is compromised by Foreign Influence factors: Mandarin Chinese fluency, State Department Gilman Scholarship to study in China, Chinese Language and Culture Club VP, Chinese Department Senior Scholarship. Not an automatic disqualifier for Secret, but TS/SCI is unlikely. Plan around **roles that do not require clearance** — most commercial/BD/strategy roles at defense-adjacent companies do not.
- The marriage year (2026) and ACE parallel track are constraints on bandwidth. 6–12 month search is realistic; don't sacrifice ACE momentum for the search.

## Why Traditional B2B SaaS Is No Longer the Primary Target

As of early 2026, the B2B SaaS sector is undergoing a structural correction. Key data points:

- iShares Software ETF down **21%+ YTD** in early 2026
- Estimated **$2 trillion in SaaS market cap erased**
- **40% of enterprise IT budgets** actively shifting from SaaS subscriptions to agentic/LLM platforms
- Software forward P/E multiples collapsed from 84x (2020–2022 peak) to **22.7x** by March 2026
- Forrester declared: *"SaaS as we know it is dead"*

The mechanism: AI agents are replacing the human workflows that justified per-seat SaaS pricing. Traditional "Director of Customer Success at a SaaS company" is itself a disruption target. The CS teams that once managed 200 accounts with 20 people are being restructured around AI tooling. Equity at a growth-stage SaaS company that is not AI-native is speculative at best.

**Conclusion:** De-prioritize traditional B2B SaaS (Gong, ZoomInfo, Braze, etc.) as primary targets. Re-weight toward AI-native companies, Big 4 AI practices, defense tech (commercial), and enterprises doing genuine AI transformation.

## Role Recommendation

### Primary: AI Implementation / Strategy at an AI-Native or Defense-Adjacent Company

Conley's real differentiator is that he has **actually built AI systems in production** — multi-agent orchestration (n8n, LangGraph, OpenClaw), HITL review pipelines, data enrichment waterfalls, content automation. Most people applying to "AI" roles have used ChatGPT. He has built systems. That gap is enormous in 2026.

Target titles:
- **Senior AI Strategist / Director of AI Strategy** — at a consulting firm (Big 4), enterprise, or defense company
- **Deployment Strategist / Commercial Implementation Lead** — at Palantir (commercial side, no clearance required), or equivalent AI deployment firm
- **Head of AI / AI Transformation Lead** — at a non-tech enterprise (healthcare, defense, financial services) building an internal AI capability
- **Director of Client Success** — at an AI-native company (Anthropic commercial, Cohere, etc.) where CS requires real technical depth

### Secondary: Big 4 AI Practice

Deloitte, Accenture, PwC, and KPMG are aggressively building AI practices. These roles:
- Don't require clearance
- Pay $120–165K base at senior associate / manager level
- Have real upward mobility (Manager → Senior Manager → Director → Partner)
- Offer fascinating client mix (Fortune 500 AI transformation projects)
- Build exactly the enterprise consulting network that makes a future venture more fundable
- Accenture and Deloitte have large Philadelphia presences

The risk: Big 4 culture is demanding and can be grind-heavy. Hybrid is standard but may not be fully remote.

### Why Not Palantir "Head of AI"

Palantir is one of the most selective technology companies in the world. They hire exceptional FAANG-level engineers, decorated military officers, and top MBA graduates. "Head of AI" is not a realistic near-term target. What **is** realistic:

- **Deployment Strategist** (Palantir commercial, no clearance): On-site with commercial clients building and deploying AIP solutions. Requires technical depth + client management. Conley's BA experience + actual AI builds make this a plausible stretch target.
- **Commercial AE or BD roles** at Palantir are less interesting and less technically aligned.

If Drone Enterprises is a serious near-term priority, a Palantir commercial role would accelerate it: direct exposure to DoD procurement patterns, SBIR/OTA fluency, and the network of people who know how defense acquisition actually works.

## The 6–12 Month Execution Plan

### Months 1–3: Foundation

1. **Rebuild LinkedIn** — reposition as "AI implementation and client success leader." New headline, new about section, new featured section. Surface the AI systems work without revealing ACE details: *"Building multi-agent automation systems independently alongside my day job."* This is true and impressive.
2. **Activate the Thought Leader Engine for LinkedIn** — 3–4 posts per week on AI in enterprise, real implementation stories, CS operations and AI, defense technology. This is not a vanity exercise — it creates inbound from recruiters at target companies and warms up hiring managers before applications land.
3. **New resume variant** — the CS + AI leadership variant (see [[resume-cs-ai-leadership-2026]]). Includes BA role with correct title, ACE-adjacent AI systems work, updated skills.
4. **Build target list** — 20 companies, named hiring manager or VP at each. Begin connecting on LinkedIn.

### Months 2–5: Pipeline

5. **Apply to first 8–12 roles** — tailored materials per target. Use the resume variant and a tailored cover note (not a generic cover letter — a 3-sentence positioning statement via email).
6. **First-round conversations** — treat every screening call as intelligence, not just an audition. Learn what the role actually is, what pain the team has, what success looks like.
7. **Continue content cadence** — do not stop posting during interviews. Active LinkedIn presence is visible to hiring teams and creates signal.
8. **Leverage ACE outreach infrastructure** — the Thought Leader Engine outreach agent can draft warm intro messages to named contacts at target companies. Use it.

### Months 4–12: Close

9. **Narrow to 3–5 real opportunities.** Don't apply broadly past this point — go deep on the right ones.
10. **Negotiate hard.** Counter-leverage: current income + ACE side income + clear in-demand skill set. The AI implementation skills are rare. Do not accept the first number.
11. **Target: $130–150K base at hire**, with documented path to $150–175K+ at first performance cycle.

## What the Corporate Stint Should Build

The job is not the destination — it's 3–4 years of deliberate capital accumulation across four dimensions:

| Dimension | Target | How |
|---|---|---|
| Financial | $400–600K saved + equity events | High comp + discipline; equity from a well-chosen company |
| Network | 50–100 decision-makers in the venture's target domain | Active LinkedIn + in-person events; be the person who connects others |
| Credentials | VP-level title + AI implementation track record | Rise fast; take on stretch projects; build visible output |
| Domain expertise | Deep knowledge of whatever industry the venture operates in | Choose the job to match the venture — defense, healthcare, AI tooling |

If Drone Enterprises is the exit, the job should be in defense-adjacent territory. If ACE/Proactive Ghost is the exit, the job should be in AI tooling or marketing technology (but AI-native, not legacy SaaS). If the venture is still undetermined, Big 4 consulting provides the most optionality.

## Relationship to ACE and 2026 Doctrine

The 2026 Operating Doctrine says "No plan to exit BA in 2026 (or likely 2027)." This career move modifies that stance — intentionally. The doctrine's primary objective is *"Financial security with an expansive arc"* and *"Decisions made from stability, not urgency."* A 30–60% compensation increase to a remote or hybrid role that funds the eventual venture is consistent with the spirit of the doctrine even if it changes the specific plan.

**What should not change:**
- ACE continues at current cadence during the search. Do not let job searching consume ACE momentum.
- LinkedIn content is additive to the thought leader engine, not a replacement for it.
- The wedding (June 12, 2026) is not affected. The search is a 6–12 month runway, not a sprint.
