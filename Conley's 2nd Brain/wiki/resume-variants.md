# Resume Variants

**Related:** [[conley-potter]], [[career-history]], [[portfolio-website]], [[brand-voice]]

---

## Overview

Conley maintains three distinct resume variants, all dated July 2025. The base content is identical — same five roles, same education, same skills list — but the Summary and Core Competencies sections are rewritten per target audience. This is a classic resume targeting strategy: one master chronology, multiple positioning lenses.

These resumes represent the day-job/career-search identity — not the ACE/freelance persona. They would be used for FTE applications or high-stakes contract engagements, not Upwork. See [[conley-potter]] for the ACE/portfolio persona distinction.

### 3. ADS — Performance Marketing & Paid Media Strategist

**File:** `Conley-Potter-Resume-July-2025-ADS.md`  
**Target:** eCommerce brands, DTC companies, performance marketing agencies  
**Experience claim:** 4+ years  

**Summary positioning:** "Data-driven digital marketing strategist with 4+ years of experience managing full-funnel paid media campaigns across Meta, Google, and TikTok... ROAS-focused experimentation for eCommerce brands."

**Core competencies framed around:**
- Paid Social & Search Strategy (Meta, Google, TikTok)
- Performance Marketing & ROAS Optimization
- Data Feed Management & Google Shopping
- A/B Testing & Creative Experimentation
- Audience Segmentation & Funnel Targeting

**When to use:** Applications to eCommerce brands or agencies where paid media execution is the primary deliverable. The narrower "4+ years" claim is intentional — this variant focuses specifically on paid media tenure rather than total marketing experience.

> **Note on the "4+ vs 7+" discrepancy:** The ADS variant says "4+ years" while the other two say "7+." This is intentional positioning, not an error — "4+ years" refers specifically to hands-on paid media management (Meta/Google/TikTok), which is a narrower slice of the 7-year overall career. The broader career includes roles (e.g., Tenfold, Stoltzfus Structures) where paid media was not the primary deliverable.
