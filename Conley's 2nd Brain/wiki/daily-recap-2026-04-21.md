## What This Reveals

**Viktor is already earning its keep on day one.** Using an AI tool to pull MCC-level Google Ads metrics in real time before a client call — and having those metrics be correct — is a meaningful operational upgrade. The quip about "gunning for Adam Hicks' job" is a joke, but it signals something real: Viktor can do the data-pull work that previously required a human to be in the ad accounts. If this scales across account prep, it compresses Conley's pre-call time significantly.

**Sage Creek's AI platform visibility (ChatGPT + Gemini) is a leading-edge metric.** This is the first time an AutoBoost client has been documented appearing in AI search results with tracked user counts. Sixteen AI-platform users in a single month for a local auto repair shop is notable. This may become a reporting standard across all BA clients as AI search adoption grows — and it's a selling point BA should surface to prospects.

**The DSA deprecation affects BA's entire Google Ads client base.** Every client running DSA campaigns will need to migrate to AI Max. This is an opportunity (proactive BA consultation) and a risk (campaign disruption if not managed). Conley surfaced this to Jon immediately, which is the right move. Worth tracking how BA handles the migration across accounts.

**Salta's formal review happened without drama.** Given the Apr 15 flag ("poor performance"), a formal monthly review on Apr 21 is the correct response. Whether the review resolved anything isn't clear from the recap — the recording will have the answer. If performance is genuinely weak and there's no turnaround plan, this client may become a churn risk.

**Rantz Automotive's LSA addition vs. GA reduction is a pattern to watch.** When clients shift budget from GA to LSA, the overall ad spend stays similar but the channel mix changes. GA gives BA more optimization levers; LSA is more Google-managed. Conley's instinct to keep both running is sound — this is the same logic as the Jumpstart/Jumpstart+ structure. Jordan Bania trying to maintain the GA budget from the AV side suggests AV also has a stake in how clients allocate.

**Claude Teams hitting its monthly cap is a signal of real adoption.** A 13-person agency burning through its Claude Teams monthly usage limit suggests AI tools are genuinely embedded in BA's workflow — not just experimented with. This is relevant context for ACE's positioning: BA's own AI adoption demonstrates the market Conley is selling into.
