## Technical contributions

From a meeting between Conley and Sam (documented in the DENT meeting notes), Sam surfaced several specific materials science and electronic warfare concepts relevant to Drone Enterprises' build strategy:

### Faraday cage / EMF protection
- Proposed using a **Faraday cage** integrated into the drone airframe to protect against **EMF-based attacks** (electronic warfare countermeasures)
- The cage would serve a dual purpose: it eliminates **thermal resonance signatures**, making the drone harder to detect and lock onto via infrared/thermal targeting systems
- Also discussed an **EMF "ray gun"** concept — a directed-energy countermeasure that could be mounted on or deployed alongside drone platforms

### Materials and coatings
- **Nickel coating paint** — applied to exterior surfaces for weather resistance and RF (radio frequency) shielding properties; nickel is both conductive (useful for Faraday cage integration) and corrosion-resistant
- **PETG outer shell** — a thermoplastic material for the drone's external body; PETG offers impact resistance, chemical resistance, and is suitable for thermoforming into aerodynamic shapes; lighter and more manufacturable than carbon fiber at scale
- **Carbon coating** — an additional surface treatment for stealth and durability; carbon-based coatings can absorb radar signatures and provide thermal protection

### Significance

These details go beyond the high-level founding thesis documented in [[drone-enterprises]] and represent Sam's specific technical contribution to the hardware architecture conversation. The Faraday cage concept in particular bridges two requirements from the [[drone-build-strategy]]: survivability (EMF protection) and stealth (thermal signature reduction). The materials choices (PETG + nickel + carbon) suggest a practical, cost-conscious approach to achieving military-grade protection without exotic composites.
