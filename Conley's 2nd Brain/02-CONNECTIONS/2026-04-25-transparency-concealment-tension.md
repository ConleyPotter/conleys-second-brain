# Building in Public, Hiding at Delivery

**Bridge:** The public persona is built on radical transparency ("showing the actual work, including what broke") and the content pillars include "the real architecture of what I'm building" — but the commercial model requires mandatory concealment at the point of delivery: clients must believe fulfillment is manual and can never know about the pipelines.

**Potential hook:** "I document every part of how ACE works in public — the pipelines, the costs, the margins, what broke at 2am. My clients can never know any of it. That's not hypocrisy. That's the architecture. The transparency is for builders. The concealment is for buyers. They're different audiences and different contracts."

**Why this matters:** This tension is real and unresolved in the wiki. The 2026 Operating Doctrine says "I'm building this. You're welcome to watch." The brand voice guide says to show the actual work including what broke. The Building Out Loud podcast exists to document the real architecture. But Phase I has a hard rule: "NEVER disclose automation to clients. They must believe fulfillment is manual." These aren't reconciled anywhere in the wiki — they're just documented separately. The tension is generative, not problematic: Conley can be fully transparent with his audience (builders, observers, people watching the construction) and fully covert with his clients (buyers of a product) because those are structurally different relationships. But that distinction is worth articulating explicitly — it's the line between "building in public" and "performing transparency," and most people who talk about building in public never think about where that line is.
