# The Digital-Funds-Real Architecture Appears Twice

**Bridge:** Both ACE and The River Room use the same internal financial design: a cheap, high-margin digital operation funds a harder, slower, more capital-intensive "real" thing — the design pattern isn't just a funding relationship between them, it's the same structural principle applied independently inside each project.

**Potential hook:** "Every project I care about has the same financial architecture: build a machine that prints at 90% margin, then spend the proceeds on the thing that actually matters. ACE does this for The Sentinel and The River Room. The River Room does it internally — the Workshop funds the Commons. Same structure, different scale."

- [[ace-overview]]

**Why this matters:** The obvious read is that ACE funds The River Room (the wiki states this). The non-obvious read is that The River Room's V2 business plan has the same internal architecture as ACE: a low-cost digital operation (online workshops, corporate training) generates surplus that makes the physical space viable without requiring it to be profitable from day one. The River Room plan explicitly names this: "the digital business funds and de-risks the physical one." That's the same mental model that produced ACE's phase structure — Phase I generates runway that funds Phase II, which generates the margin that makes Phase III possible. Conley designed both systems with the same logic. That logic is worth naming: **cheap computation subsidizes meaningful complexity**. It might be his actual operating principle, not just a financial tactic.
